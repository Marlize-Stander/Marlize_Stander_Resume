Blog

My Dev Journey so far - Tales from a newbie

If anyone told me a year ago, that I would be busy studying again, let alone to become a Web Developer, I would have called you crazy! Well, at the age of 42, let me tell you, it is no joke! I was never one to back down from a challenge before, and as soon as I realized how hard this was, I was seriously considering becoming a quiter.. But being the control-freak I am, and always wanting to prove everything to everyone, I threw myself into this thing wholeheartedly, some days with less bravado than others, but keeping my chin up and pushing through!

This is not a fancy blog, and I am not pretending to be a writer or anything, but merely trying to help others in similar "sticky" shoes, with some advice and tips I have gathered so far on my journey:

You are NEVER too old to learn anything new! Even though you sometimes feel it, do not give up, believe in yourself.


Do not become stuck in TUTORIAL HELL - yes it is actually a thing! It can become very overwhelming when you are on the web 24/7. Start with the basics and make sure you take it one step at a time.


You will NEVER get to learn everything in 3 months, take your time, and make sure you stay motivated. Everyone's journey is unique, do not compare yourself with others.


NOBODY knows everything! And neither do they expect this from you! It is OK to google for answers sometimes! This does not make you a cheater, you are learning from others.


ASK for help when you need it! And do not see this as criticism, take it in your stride, fix it, learn from it, and see if you can improve it!
